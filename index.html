01 · About

A small signal.
A big question.

We study how the brain integrates sensory data with prior knowledge — and what that interplay reveals about perception, behavior, and the neural mechanisms underlying choice.

Shannon Saszik, Ph.D. is an Associate Professor and Acting Chair of the Department of Psychology at Northeastern Illinois University in Chicago. She leads the School of Thought, a neuropsychology lab investigating how bottom-up sensory signals and top-down knowledge signals interact through parallel brain pathways — and how reliance on one system over the other shifts to shape behavior and choice.

Her career spans three decades of research: from foundational work characterizing zebrafish retinal physiology and development using the electroretinogram (ERG), to mammalian retinal electrophysiology at Northwestern University's Feinberg School of Medicine, to her current focus on the neural and behavioral mechanisms of perception and decision-making. She is a co-investigator on an NSF-funded STEM learning community supporting students at NEIU, a federally recognized Hispanic-Serving Institution, and a contributor to NEIU's M.A. in Human Developmental Science program.

"My research interests focus on examination of the processing of information in the brain through parallel pathways, and ultimately how that processing impacts behavior and choice."

02 · Research

Five questions.
One brain.

Our projects converge on a single question: how does the brain decide what to trust — the raw data streaming in from the senses, or the knowledge accumulated through a lifetime of experience?

P-01 · Flagship

Bottom-up sensation & top-down knowledge

How do data signals coded by peripheral receptors interact with knowledge signals shaped by experience and memory? The School of Thought uses behavioral paradigms, psychophysical methods, and electrophysiological recording to map this interaction at multiple levels — from photoreceptor to decision.

NeuropsychologySensation & PerceptionElectrophysiologyBehavioral paradigms
P-02

Zebrafish visual neuroscience

Using the Danio rerio ERG to characterize photoreceptor contributions to vision across development — and how temperature, light history, and ethanol exposure shape retinal function.

ERGDanio rerioPhotoreceptors
P-03

Mammalian retinal electrophysiology

Some bipolar cells use Na⁺ action potentials alongside graded responses to encode rapid changes in illumination — patch-clamp work in ground squirrel and mouse revealing novel retinal circuits.

Bipolar cellsNa⁺ spikesPatch-clamp
P-04

Stress, dopamine & social behavior

Chronic stress and dopamine disruption reshape social behavior in adult zebrafish — bridging physiology and behavioral neuropsychology.

P-05 · Applied

Decision-making & signal integration

How do physiological state and environmental context shift reliance on sensory vs. memory-based signals in behavioral choice? Current work examines this across stress, arousal, and aging conditions.
